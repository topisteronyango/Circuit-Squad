National Healthcare Integration System (NHIS)

NHIS aims to provide seamless and coordinated care to patients, which can lead to better health outcomes, increased patient satisfaction, and reduced healthcare costs. The integration of services across different healthcare settings can improve the continuity of care, reduce duplication of services, and enhance communication between healthcare providers, ultimately resulting in better health outcomes for patients.

OUR SERVICES

Doctors

Our system enables enrolled doctors to access and upload health records of their patients directly from the patient directory..

Patients

Our platform offers patients the ability to search, contact, and schedule appointments with doctors through our doctor directory.

Insurance

We have established partnerships with a majority of insurance providers to ensure that patients have uninterrupted access to medical care..

BENEFITS OF NHIS
Interoperability
Centralized and integrated healthcare management system for all hospitals which builds a single uniform record for each patient thus minimizing duplicated efforts and potential health risks for patients.
optimization
Streamlined patient care processes, from appointment scheduling to billing leading to increased efficiency and revenue growth for healthcare providers and hospitals.
digitization
Elimination of paper records which are prone to damage and security risks and helps to save costs, save on space and best of all save the planet.
interconnectivity
Real-time access to critical patient information for authorized healthcare providers and ensure that medical professionals have access to complete and up-to-date information, regardless of where a patient has received treatment in the past.
analytics
Valuable insights into patient health and healthcare utilization for informed decision making and continuous improvement of healthcare services by the Ministry of Health.
integration
Linkage with the National Health Insurance Fund and other Health Insurance Firms to eliminate lengthy visits and paperwork for clearance thus increasing efficiency in health delivery.
coordination
Linkage with emergency services to provide faster and more accurate emergency response, as healthcare providers will have access to patient information and medical history, allowing for better informed decision making in emergency situations.
forensic investigation
Linkage with forensic investigation agencies to aid in investigations by providing a comprehensive record of a patient's medical history and treatments received, potentially aiding in the identification and prosecution of perpetrators of crimes.
